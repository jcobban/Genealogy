The images of the 1861, 1871, 1881, and 1891 censuses of Canada are not yet available on the web.  They will almost certainly be available in less than 2 years.

 

There is a heads of household index for the 1871 census at

http://www.collectionscanada.ca/archivianet/index-e.html

 

Of course the 1881 census is indexed at http://www.familysearch.org/ although the quality is not good.  I personally spent over 100 hours proofreading this index before it was made available and yet still over 10% of the names are seriously mangled.  Frequently I have to fall back on the CD version and do a search of a district by first name only (which the web site does not support).

 

The images of the 1851, 1901, 1906 and 1911 censuses of  Canada are available for free at http://www.collectionscanada.ca/archivianet/index-e.html organized geographically.

 

Nominal indices of the 1901, 1906, and 1911 censuses are available at http://www.automatedgenealogy.com/census/index.html.  The 1901 index is complete, 1906 is 85% complete, 1911 is 73% complete.

 

A second transcription project for the 1906 census is 63% complete at http://www.afhs.ab.ca/data/census/1906/new.html#progress
