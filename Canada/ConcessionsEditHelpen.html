Edit Concessions: Help

This page presents a list of all of the concessions into which a township is divided for the purpose of describing the location of properties. Across most of North America rural areas were assigned in terms of "sections" or "lots" each of 800 acres, identified by a concession and a lot number running from one end of the concession to the other. This page permits adding, deleting, and modifying the descriptions of individual concessions. For each concession there is a row in the table containing:

  • A unique string which is used to identify the concession. Most commonly concessions were simply numbered and such concessions are identified in this table by the string "con " plus the number. However other frequent concession names are: Broken Front, represented by the string "BF", which is used where one boundary of the township was a river or lakeshore; North or East or South or West Boundary, represented as "NB", "EB", "SB", or "WB". numbered ranges on either side of a baseline road, for example "1RN" or "3RS" or "SNBTR", etc.
  • The latitude of the centre of lot 1 of the concession.
  • The longitude of the centre of lot 1 of the concession.
  • The increment (or decrement if negative) of latitude when the lot number increases by 1.
  • The increment (or decrement if negative) of longitude when the lot number increases by 1.
  • A Delete button to delete the concession.
  • An Add button to insert a concession or concessions before or after a specific concession.

Note that the order of concessions is significant if the concessions are parallel to each other, which is the most common situation, as some of the tools for assisting the administer to initialize the latitude and longitude of lot 1 of the concessions assume that the concessions are in order.
